Setting up custom fonts in Zoho online products varies depending on the specific application you are using. Generally, you can access your brand's unique custom fonts or Google Fonts directly through the built-in Font Library or by adding the font family via Custom CSS. [1, 2, 3] 
Here is a quick, app-specific breakdown for the most popular Zoho products:
## 1. Zoho Writer

* Organization-wide: Admins can add fonts from their local devices by navigating to Admin Panel > Configuration > Font Settings and uploading TrueType Font (.ttf) files.
* Font Sets: Go to the Design tab > Font Set > Add Font Set. Choose fonts for your Headings and Body, then save. [2, 4, 5, 6] 

## 2. Zoho Sites

* Adding Custom Fonts: Click the hamburger menu, go to Manage > Fonts > Custom Fonts > Add Font Family. Enter the name and upload your .ttf files. [3] 
* Applying to Elements: Because custom fonts may not show in the visual editor, you must add the font name to the CSS section of the HTML/CSS Editor. [1, 7] 

## 3. Zoho CRM

* Email Composer: Navigate to Setup > Channels > Email > Email Configuration > Compose > Default Font Family and select Add Custom Font (you can also just type in a custom font name that is locally installed on your computer).
* Note: Custom fonts are not yet available in the standard drag-and-drop Template Builder. [8, 9] 

## 4. Zoho Forms

* Web Fonts: In the form builder, click the Font Family dropdown and select Manage Fonts.
* Font Library: Hover over any Google Font in the pop-up and click the + icon to add it to your font list. [10, 11] 

## 5. Zoho Show & Zoho Sheet

* Zoho Show: From the editor, click on any text box to open the Format pane on the right. Click the Font dropdown, select Manage Fonts, and add fonts from the library or enable Font Library for your Team.
* Zoho Sheet: Select the text, click the Font Style dropdown in the toolbar, and browse the updated font library to add preferred styles. [6, 12, 13, 14, 15] 

## 6. Zoho Books & Zoho Invoice

* PDF Templates: Go to Settings > Templates > PDF Templates. Click Edit on a specific template and navigate to the General section in the left sidebar to change your font. [16, 17]
